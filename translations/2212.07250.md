# Affine Monads and Lazy Structures for Bayesian Programming
## الموناد الأفيني والبنى الكسولة للبرمجة البايزية

**arXiv ID:** 2212.07250
**Authors:** Swaraj Dash, Younesse Kaddar, Hugo Paquet, Sam Staton
**Year:** 2022
**Categories:** cs.PL
**Translation Quality:** 0.88
**Glossary Terms Used:** monad, data structure, probability, inference

### English Abstract
The authors demonstrate that "streams and lazy data structures are a natural idiom for programming with infinite-dimensional Bayesian methods" including Poisson processes, Gaussian processes, and Dirichlet processes. Their approach employs two distinct monads: an affine monad supporting laziness and a commutative non-affine monad for measures. They verify this separation matters for decidability and that quasi-Borel spaces support both monads. The work introduces specialized inference methods adapted to laziness, validated through the Metropolis-Hastings-Green method, and presents implementation as a Haskell library called LazyPPL.
