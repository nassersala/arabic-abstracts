### English Abstract
This research explores how large language models can be integrated into multi-agent simulations by substituting conventional agent programming with LLM-driven prompts. The study demonstrates this approach using two complex systems from swarm intelligence: ant colony foraging and bird flocking. The authors developed a toolchain combining LLMs with NetLogo simulation platform via Python extension, enabling communication with GPT-4o through the OpenAI API. The system supports both "structured, rule-based prompts and autonomous, knowledge-driven prompts." Results indicate that this toolchain allows LLMs to investigate self-organizing processes and generate emergent behaviors in multi-agent environments, offering novel approaches to modeling intelligent systems inspired by natural phenomena. The researchers have made their code, simulations, and datasets publicly available.
