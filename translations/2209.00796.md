### English Abstract
Diffusion models are a class of likelihood-based generative models that are easy to train and capable of generating high-quality samples. They have recently gained significant attention in both the machine learning and computer vision communities, leading to a surge of new research directions and applications. This survey organizes the literature on diffusion models into three main categories: efficient sampling, improved likelihood estimation, and handling data with special structures. For each category, we provide a comprehensive review of the literature while highlighting the connections between diffusion models and other types of deep generative models. We also summarize applications of diffusion models in various domains, including computer vision, natural language processing, temporal data modeling, multi-modal modeling, molecular graph generation, anomaly detection, and other scientific applications. Finally, we discuss several promising future directions. Our taxonomy of 396 papers is publicly available at https://github.com/YangLing0818/Diffusion-Models-Papers-Survey-Taxonomy.
