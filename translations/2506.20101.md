# Secure Multi-Key Homomorphic Encryption with Application to Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning
## التشفير المتماثل الآمن متعدد المفاتيح مع تطبيق على التعلم الموحد الحافظ للخصوصية

**arXiv ID:** 2506.20101

**Year:** 2025
**Categories:** cs.CR
**Translation Quality:** 0.92
**Glossary Terms Used:** homomorphic encryption, multi-key, security, privacy, machine learning, implementation, algorithm

### English Abstract
The researchers identify a critical security flaw in existing multi-key homomorphic encryption schemes (CDKS) that may inadvertently leak plaintext information from one party to others. To address this vulnerability, they introduce SMHE, a new scheme that adds a novel masking mechanism to protect confidentiality during computation across multiple data providers. Their privacy-preserving federated learning implementation demonstrates that SMHE offers substantially enhanced security with minimal performance impact—specifically, less than a 2× runtime and communication traffic increase compared to the CDKS-based approach. The authors have made their implementation publicly available on GitHub.
