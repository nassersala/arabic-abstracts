### English Abstract
This survey examines how Large Language Models function within multi-agent systems that work together to solve problems. The research introduces a framework analyzing collaboration through five dimensions: the participants involved, interaction types (such as cooperation or competition), organizational patterns (peer networks or centralized models), coordination methods (including role or model-based approaches), and management protocols. The authors review existing approaches to enable agents to "coordinate and solve complex tasks collectively" and explore practical applications spanning telecommunications, manufacturing, information retrieval, and cultural domains. The work concludes by discussing lessons learned and identifying future research opportunities "towards artificial collective intelligence."
