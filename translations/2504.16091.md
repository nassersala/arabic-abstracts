# Post-Quantum Homomorphic Encryption: A Case for Code-Based Alternatives

### English Abstract
The paper explores how homomorphic encryption allows secure computation on encrypted data without decryption. Since quantum computing threatens traditional encryption methods, the authors examine post-quantum alternatives. While most current research focuses on lattice-based approaches, this work advocates for exploring error-correcting code-based solutions instead. The authors detail the mathematical foundations of code-based cryptography, compare it with lattice-based methods, identify obstacles limiting code-based progress, and propose five research directions to advance this field.
