### English Abstract
Deep learning, despite its remarkable achievements, is still a young field. Like the early stages of many scientific disciplines, it is marked by the discovery of new phenomena, ad-hoc design decisions, and the lack of a uniform and compositional mathematical foundation. This thesis develops a category-theoretic mathematical foundation for deep learning. It introduces parametric weighted optics as a model for neural networks, provides lens-theoretic axiomatization of differentiation covering both smooth and discrete settings, surveys categorical models of architectures, formalizes optimizers, and combines these concepts into a compositional framework for supervised learning.
