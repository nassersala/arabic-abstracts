# Controllable Image Synthesis of Industrial Data Using Stable Diffusion

### English Abstract
In this work, we address the problem of how to obtain large annotated datasets for industrial applications, taking defect detection in manufacturing as a case study. By leveraging recent generative models such as Stable Diffusion, we are able to learn the distribution of the industrial data and synthesize defective images together with their annotations. Our approach enables controlling the topological properties of the generated masks while preserving the background of the original images. This allows us to learn where defects can occur. We demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach on a crack segmentation task, where we show a considerable performance increase under several metrics, especially when real-world training data is scarce.
