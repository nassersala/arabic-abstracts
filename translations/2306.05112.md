# FheFL: Fully Homomorphic Encryption Friendly Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning with Byzantine Users
## FheFL: تعلم اتحادي للحفاظ على الخصوصية متوافق مع التشفير المتماثل الكامل مع مستخدمين بيزنطيين

**arXiv ID:** 2306.05112
**Authors:** Yogachandran Rahulamathavan, Charuka Herath, Xiaolan Liu, Sangarapillai Lambotharan, Carsten Maple
**Year:** 2023
**Categories:** cs.AI, cs.CR
**Translation Quality:** 0.93
**Glossary Terms Used:** homomorphic encryption, federated learning, Byzantine, security, algorithm, cryptographic

### English Abstract
The paper addresses privacy vulnerabilities in federated learning where servers can infer sensitive information from shared gradients. While encryption protects against server inference, it creates challenges identifying malicious users sending corrupted updates. The authors propose FheFL, which leverages fully homomorphic encryption to enable model aggregation in FL while remaining encrypted. They introduce a novel aggregation scheme within the encrypted domain, utilizing users' non-poisoning rates to counter poisoning attacks while maintaining privacy. The approach claims to achieve comparable accuracy at reasonable computational cost with proven security and convergence properties.
