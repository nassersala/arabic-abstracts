# Projective Dynamics: Fusing Constraint Projections for Fast Simulation
## الديناميكيات الإسقاطية: دمج إسقاطات القيود للمحاكاة السريعة

**Authors:** Sofien Bouaziz, Sebastian Martin, Tiantian Liu, Ladislav Kavan, Mark Pauly
**Year:** 2014
**Publication:** ACM Transactions on Graphics, Volume 33, Issue 4, Article 154
**DOI:** 10.1145/2601097.2601116

### English Abstract
The paper presents a new method for implicit time integration of physical systems. The approach builds a bridge between nodal Finite Element methods and Position Based Dynamics, leading to a simple, efficient, robust, yet accurate solver that supports many different types of constraints. The authors propose specially designed energy potentials that can be solved efficiently using an alternating optimization approach. Inspired by continuum mechanics, they derive a set of continuum-based potentials that can be efficiently incorporated within their solver. They demonstrate the generality and robustness of their approach in many different applications ranging from the simulation of solids, cloths, and shells, to example-based simulation. Comparisons to Newton-based and Position Based Dynamics solvers highlight the benefits of their formulation.
