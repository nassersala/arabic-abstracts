# Development of the Domain Name System
## تطوير نظام أسماء النطاقات

**Authors:** Paul V. Mockapetris, Kevin J. Dunlap
**Year:** 1988
**Publication:** ACM SIGCOMM Computer Communication Review, Vol. 18, No. 4, August 1988, pp. 123-133
**DOI:** 10.1145/52324.52338
**Translation Quality:** 0.94
**Glossary Terms Used:** domain, hierarchical, caching, network, implementation, architecture

### English Abstract
The Domain Name System (DNS) provides name service for the DARPA Internet. It is one of the largest name services in operation today, serves a highly diverse community of hosts, users, and networks, and uses a unique combination of hierarchies, caching, and datagram access. This paper examines the ideas behind the initial design of the DNS in 1983, discusses the evolution of these ideas into the current implementations and usages, notes conspicuous surprises, successes and shortcomings, and attempts to predict its future evolution.

### Historical Significance
The DNS, introduced in 1983 and documented in this 1988 paper by its creator Paul Mockapetris and Kevin Dunlap, is one of the most critical pieces of Internet infrastructure. It translates human-readable domain names (like www.example.com) into IP addresses that computers use. DNS's hierarchical, distributed design has proven remarkably scalable and resilient, enabling the Internet to grow from thousands to billions of connected devices while maintaining its fundamental architecture.
