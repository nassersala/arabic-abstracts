# Modular composition & polynomial GCD in the border of small, shallow circuits
## التركيب المعياري والقاسم المشترك الأكبر لكثيرات الحدود في حدود الدوائر الصغيرة الضحلة

**arXiv ID:** 2511.05035
**Authors:** Robert Andrews, Mrinal Kumar, Shanthanu S. Rai
**Year:** 2025
**Categories:** Computational Complexity (cs.CC); Symbolic Computation (cs.SC)
**Translation Quality:** 0.88
**Glossary Terms Used:** polynomial, algorithm, complexity, circuit, finite field

### English Abstract
The paper addresses modular composition—calculating the coefficient vector of f(g(x)) mod h(x)—and polynomial GCD computation. While finite field algorithms achieve near-linear time via Kedlaya & Umans, infinite field methods lag at O(n^1.43). The authors demonstrate that both problems can be solved using nearly-linear-size and polylogarithmic-depth algebraic circuits with division gates over any infinite field. These results advance understanding of parallel polynomial algorithms, offering progress on the open problem of achieving simultaneous polylogarithmic depth and near-linear work complexity for univariate polynomial GCD.
