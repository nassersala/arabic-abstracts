# A Tutorial Introduction to Lattice-based Cryptography and Homomorphic Encryption

### English Abstract
The paper explains why lattice-based cryptography merits study, highlighting six key reasons. The approach offers cryptosystems based on a variety of hard computational problems to avoid uniform vulnerability. It features straightforward computational aspects and lower encryption/decryption complexity compared to factorization or discrete logarithm methods. Additionally, the field provides strong worst-case hardness security proofs and represents a promising direction for post-quantum security since no known quantum algorithms substantially outperform classical approaches for lattice problems. The tutorial also emphasizes how lattice structures have enabled significant advances in homomorphic encryption research.
