# A Survey on Homomorphic Encryption Schemes: Theory and Implementation
## مسح شامل حول مخططات التشفير المتماثل: النظرية والتطبيق

**arXiv ID:** 1704.03578
**Authors:** Abbas Acar, Hidayet Aksu, A. Selcuk Uluagac, Mauro Conti
**Year:** 2017
**Categories:** cs.CR
**Translation Quality:** 0.91
**Glossary Terms Used:** homomorphic encryption, privacy, cloud computing, data, cryptographic, implementation

### English Abstract
The paper addresses privacy challenges in cloud computing and encrypted data sharing. The authors explain that homomorphic encryption allows third parties to perform operations on encrypted data without decryption. They survey the foundational concepts, including partially and somewhat homomorphic encryption schemes, then examine Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) families introduced after Craig Gentry's 2009 breakthrough. The survey covers implementations and enhancements to practical FHE systems, with the goal of providing a clear knowledge and foundation to researchers and practitioners interested in these encryption technologies.
