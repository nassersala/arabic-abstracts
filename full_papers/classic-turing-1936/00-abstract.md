# On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem
## حول الأعداد القابلة للحوسبة، مع تطبيق على مسألة القرار

**Publication:** Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society, s2-42(1):230-265, 1937
**Authors:** Alan Turing
**Year:** 1936
**Domain:** Theory of Computation
**Translation Quality:** 0.92
**Glossary Terms Used:** computable function, machine, algorithm, decimal, symbolic, mathematical problem, universal machine

### English Abstract
The computable numbers are defined as the real numbers whose expressions as a decimal are calculable by finite means. A number is computable if its decimal can be written down by a machine. Although the class of computable numbers includes all numbers which could naturally be regarded as computable (such as π, e, the real parts of algebraic numbers, and zeros of Bessel functions), they do not include all definable numbers. The paper also demonstrates that the Hilbertian Entscheidungsproblem can have no solution. The paper introduced the concept now known as the "Turing machine" - a mathematical description of a universal machine that could, in principle, solve any mathematical problem that could be presented to it in symbolic form.
