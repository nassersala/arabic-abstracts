### English Version

Our results seem to yield a solid evidence that approximating the expected optimal sparse structure by readily available dense building blocks is a viable method for improving neural networks for computer vision. The main advantage of this method is a significant quality gain at a modest increase of computational requirements compared to shallower and less wide networks. Our object detection work was competitive despite not utilizing context nor performing bounding box regression and suggests the strength of the Inception architecture. For both classification and detection, it is expected that similar quality of result can be achieved by much more expensive non-Inception-type networks of similar depth and width. Still, our approach yields solid evidence that moving to sparser architectures is feasible and useful idea in general. This suggest promising future work towards creating sparser and more refined structures in automated fashion on the basis of [2].
