## Historical Significance

This is one of the most foundational papers in computer science history. It introduced:
- **LISP**: The second-oldest high-level programming language still in widespread use (after Fortran)
- **Symbolic computation**: Processing of symbols rather than just numbers
- **Recursive function theory**: Formalism for defining functions recursively
- **S-expressions**: A notation for symbolic expressions that became fundamental
- **The apply function**: First interpreter/evaluator concept
- **List processing**: Data structures and operations on lists

The paper was designed to support the "Advice Taker" project, an early attempt at creating artificial intelligence systems that could reason with formal logic.

## Paper Structure

Unlike modern research papers, this 1960 paper follows a mathematical/tutorial style:
1. **Introduction** - Motivation from AI (Advice Taker)
2. **Functions and Function Definitions** - Foundational concepts
3. **Recursive Functions of Symbolic Expressions** - Core formalism
4. **S-expressions** - Symbolic expression notation
5. **S-functions** - Functions operating on S-expressions
6. **The LISP Programming System** - Practical implementation
7. **Representation of S-expressions** - Computer representation
8. **The Universal S-function apply** - Interpreter concept
9. **Additional sections** - Extensions and examples

**Note:** Part II of this paper was never published.
