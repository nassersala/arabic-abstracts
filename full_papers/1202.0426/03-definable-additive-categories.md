### English Version (Key Content Summary)

**Section Overview:**
This section explores the 2-category DEF of definable additive categories and interpretation functors. A category is definable if it is equivalent to a definable subcategory of a module category Mod-R, closed under direct products, direct limits, and pure subobjects.

**Main Definitions:**
- A **definable category** is a full subcategory of Mod-R closed under direct products, direct limits, and pure subobjects
- An **interpretation functor** is a functor commuting with direct products and direct limits
- A **pure monomorphism** is one whose some ultrapower is split
- An object is **pure-injective** if injective over pure monomorphisms
- The **Ziegler topology** on pinj(D) has basis {(F) = {N ∈ pinj(D) : FN ≠ 0}} for F ∈ fun(D)
- The **rep-Zariski topology** has basis [F] = (F)^c = {N ∈ pinj(D) : FN = 0}

**Examples:**
- Module categories Mod-R
- Finitely accessible additive categories with products
- Locally finitely presentable additive categories
- Category of torsion-free abelian groups
- Category of torsion abelian groups
- Category of injective abelian groups

**Theorem 3.1:** If D' is a definable subcategory of D and S_D' = {F ∈ fun(D) : FD = 0 for all D ∈ D'} is its annihilator in fun(D), then S_D' is a Serre subcategory and the quotient fun(D)/S_D is naturally equivalent to fun(D'). Every Serre subcategory arises this way.

**Theorem 3.2 (Main Equivalence):**
- If D is definable, then D ≃ Ex(fun(D), Ab)
- If A is skeletally small abelian, then A ≃ fun(Ex(A,Ab))

**Elementary Duality:**
If D = Ex(A,Ab), then the elementary dual category is D^d = Ex(A^op,Ab), and fun(D^d) = (fun(D))^op = fun^d(D).

**Proposition 3.3:** The duality fun(D) ≃ (fun(D^d))^op induces order-preserving bijections:
- Ser(fun(D)) ≃ Ser(fun(D^d))
- Sub(D) ≃ Sub(D^d)

**Theorem 3.4 (Ziegler Spectrum):** Definable subcategories of D are in natural bijection with closed subsets of its Ziegler spectrum Zg(D). Each definable subcategory is generated by the indecomposable pure-injectives in it.

**Section 3.1 - Intrinsic Definition:**
Explores whether there is an intrinsic definition of definable category independent of module categories.

**Section 3.2 - Extending from Pinj(D) to D:**
Shows that the structure of DEF is essentially determined by pure-injective subcategories.

**Main Result:** The category of pure-injective objects Pinj(D) determines the entire structure of D and the morphisms in DEF.

**Section 3.3 - Ex(A,D) is definable when D is Grothendieck:**

**Proposition 3.6:** If A is skeletally small abelian and D is locally finitely presented Grothendieck, then Ex(A,D) is definable.

**Theorem 3.9:** For any skeletally small abelian A and definable Grothendieck D:
Ex(A,D) is definable (as a subcategory of appropriate module category).
