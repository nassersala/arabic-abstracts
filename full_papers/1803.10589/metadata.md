## Paper Summary

This paper demonstrates how Gödel's first incompleteness theorem has an analog in quantum theory. The authors prove that the set of explanations fitting given experimental evidence is uncountably infinite, establishing that physics requires activities beyond computation and measurement—specifically, what they term "agency." The work has profound implications for the philosophy of physics, suggesting that no final, complete physical theory can exist.
