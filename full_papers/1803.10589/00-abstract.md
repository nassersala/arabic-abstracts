# Incompleteness theorem for physics
## نظرية عدم الاكتمال للفيزياء

**arXiv ID:** 1803.10589
**Authors:** John M. Myers, F. Hadi Madjid
**Year:** 2018
**Categories:** History and Philosophy of Physics (physics.hist-ph); Quantum Physics (quant-ph)
**Translation Quality:** 0.92
**Glossary Terms Used:** theorem, incompleteness, quantum theory, axiom, computation, measurement

### English Abstract
The authors demonstrate how Gödel's first incompleteness theorem applies to quantum theory. They argue that just as Gödel's theorem enables endless axiom expansion in arithmetic, an analogous principle exists in physics regarding evidence explanations. They establish that possible explanations for given evidence form an uncountably infinite set, demonstrating that bridging theory and experiment requires activities transcending computation and measurement—specifically, what they term "agency."
