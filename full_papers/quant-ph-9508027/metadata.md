## Paper Significance

This is one of the most influential papers in quantum computing history. Shor's algorithm demonstrated that quantum computers could solve problems (integer factorization and discrete logarithm) that are believed to be intractable for classical computers. This has profound implications for:

1. **Cryptography**: Breaking RSA and other public-key cryptosystems
2. **Complexity Theory**: Separating quantum and classical computational complexity
3. **Quantum Computing**: Providing the first killer application for quantum computers
4. **Number Theory**: Novel algorithmic approach to classical problems
